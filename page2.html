Male and Female Legends of Tennis

Throughout the years, there have been legendary, record-breaking tennis players that have lived amongst us. These legends include both males and females who have impacted tennis as we know it.


Arthur Ashe

Arthur Ashe was an American World Number One professional tennis player. He was the first African American selected to the United States Davis Cup Team and the only African American man to win the singles title at Wimbledon, the US Open, or the Australian Open. He won three Grand Slam Titles which ranked him among the best tennis players in the United States. After an astounding career, he retired in 1980.


Roger Federer

Roger Federer is a Swiss tennis player who is currently ranked World number one. Federer has won eleven Grand Slam men's singles titles, three Tennis Masters Cup titles, and 14 ATP Masters Series titles. In 2004, he became the first man since 1988 to win three of four Grand Slam singles tournaments in the same year.


Rafael Nadal

Rafael Nadal is a Spanish professional tennis player who is currently ranked World number five. Nadal has won 14 Grand Slam singles titles, the 2008 Olympic gold medal in singles, and a record 27 ATP World Tour Masters 1000 and 15 ATP World Tour 500 tournaments.


Billie Jean King

Billie Jean King was a great player who won 12 Grand Slam titles, including six at Wimbledon from 1966 to 1975. Not only was Billie Jean King an excellent player in history, she became an influencer in the fight for equal pay for men and women. In this fight for equal pay, she also fought for equal respect for women who play tennis.


Venus Williams

Venus Williams, older sister of Serena Williams, is a professional American tennis player. Williams is currently ranked number 21 in the WTA (Women's Tennis Association) singles rankings. Williams has been ranked number one three times before by the WTA, the second of all time since Althea Gibson. She has won 14 Grand Slam doubles titles, all of which have been played with Serena Williams. WIlliams has also won four Olympic gold medals, one in singles and three in women's doubles.


Serena Williams

Serena Williams is a professional American tennis player. The WTA has ranked her world number one on eight occasions. Williams holds the most Grand Slam titles in singles, doubles, and mixed doubles, and holds an all-time record for the most women's singles matches won at majors with 331 matches.
