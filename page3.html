Tennis Tournaments

There are various tennis tournaments that take place around the world. There are many tournaments, however, of a greater important to a professional tennis player's career. While there is an abundance of tournaments, there are some that are highly competitive, especially for professional players.


Grand Slam Tournaments

There are four tournaments that are involved in the official Grand Slam tournament. In chronological order, it is as follows: Australian Open, French Open, Wimbledon, and the U.S. Open.


Australian Open

The Australian Open is the first of the four Grand Slam tennis tournaments held annually in Melbourne, Australia. After its first event in 1905, the Australian Open is currently the largest annual sporting event in the Southern hemisphere, holding the record for the highest attendance at a Grand Slam event.


French Open

The French Open, also called the Roland-Garros, is a major tennis event held in Paris, France. Unlike the other Grand Slam events that are held on grass courts or hardcourts, the French Open is held on clay courts, making it a slow playing surface. Due to the intensity of the regulation matches and the playing surface, it is considered the most physically demanding tennis tournament in the world.


Wimbledon

Wimbledon is the oldest tennis tournament in the world and has been held at Wimbledon, London since 1877. The tournament takes place in an outdoor grass court. After the Australian Open swithced to hardcourt in 1988, Wimbledon is the only major tennis tournament played on grass. There are four junior events and five main events at Wimbledon, the main events being Gentlemens' singles, Ladies' singles, Gentlemen's doubles, Ladies' doubles, and mixed doubles.


US Open

The US Open tournament is a modern version of one of the oldest tennis championships in the world, the U.S. National Championship, where men's singles was played in 1881. Since 1978, the tournament has been played at the USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center in Queens, New York. The U.S. Open is owned and organized by the Unted States Tennis Association, a non-profit organization.
