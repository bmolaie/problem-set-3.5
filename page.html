Scoring Terminology

There are certain words or phrases used during matches that may appear alien to those not associated with tennis.

  • Scoring in tennis is as such: 0, 15, 30, 40
  • Love: When the score is at 0
  • All: When the score is tied (cannot be applied for a deuce)
  • Deuce: When both players are tied at 40-40. A deuce can be ended when a player scores two more consecutive points
  • Ad-in or advantage: Used when players are in a deuce and the server needs only one more point to win
  • Ad-out or disadvantage: Used when players are ina deuce and the reciever needs only one more point to win
  • Singles: One on one
  • Doubles: Two on two
  • Fault: Occurs on a serve when the ball does not land in the correct service box or hits the net
  • Let: When the ball hits the net but goes over into the opponent's side of the court

Rules

There are rules and regulations that must be followed in matches.

  1. If the ball lands on the line, it is counted in
  2. The first serve must always start on the right hand side and served overhand into the diagonal service box
  3. The score of the person serving is announced first, then the score of the opponent, followed by "first serve" or "second serve" depending if a fault was used during that game
  4. In a singles match, both players switch to the other baseline side after each point is scored
  5. In a doubles match, the players that are serving switch baselines while the recievers remain stationary
  6. Players switch to the opposite side of the court when the total games played is odd
  7. A serve can be returned only after it hits the ground once, but after the serve is returned, the ball can be hit in the air
  8. The ball can only hit the ground once. If it bounces twice, the point goes to the opposing player
  9. The server is allowed one two faults on each serve. If the server messes up both faults, the point is awarded to the opponent
